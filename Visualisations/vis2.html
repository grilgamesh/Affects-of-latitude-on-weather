Latitude against Humidity

An interesting pattern, but not one we can learn much from. clearly there is no link between latitude and Humidity.

However, the gap in the lower right of the graph is interesting. it shows that we found no cities in the southern hemisphere with very low humidity.

There might be very interesting reasons around this. Are there more coastal cities in the southern hemispehere, just due to the shapes of the landmasses?

Also, the data are quite sparse in the centre, implying that equatorial regions are very humid. This seems realistic.
